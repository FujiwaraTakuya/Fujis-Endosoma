Year: 1200, Set on a planet devoid of humans, Pokemon society eventually gradually advanced past old tribal cultures and have begun to grow steadily as a medieval-like society. As populations began to skyrocket with the growing societal changes, food, water, and living spaces began to grow sparse as civilian discontent grew at large and fights began to break out between species of all kinds; from the homeowners to the nomads, to the hermits and the powerful land-claimers still clinging to old measures of might makes right. Because of these differences, it all eventually culminated to civil wars amongst all Pokemon whether they liked it or not- fighting over territory, the right to eat, the right to live, even personal philosophies. Millions were dying by the year, most could not see eye to eye from each other, and the world was also ravaged by pillages and kidnappings amidst all of the unfair carnage and hatred that was brought about. It was only after 20 years of wanton wars that Arceus, God of the Poke-universe could no longer abide by their passive creed and thus made it their mission to save all Pokemon from themselves.

Arceus gave birth to more legendary Pokémon under the guidance of the existing legendary Pokémon and imbued each leader with a legendary power- The Crest of Arceus, each individually giving the user more power than they already have, alongside a tertiary Typing in hopes to help mitigate their harder to battle enemies; the mark being visible upon a part of their skin akin to a tattoo that displays the golden Ring of Arceus. He instructed every and all legendary Pokemon to make themselves known to the Pokemon of the world and protect them from themselves and solve their issues and disputes. Under his guidance, the legendary Pokemon set out to right the wrongs of the world and protect all from harm, and within 100 years, was able to prevent any preventable deaths from occurring with the aid of the giant number of Legendary Pokemon now roaming the earth. They were given the title: Arceus Guardian

During the war, the people began to notice that Arceus' power was leaking to other Pokemon as non-Alpha Pokemon began appearing with strengths and resistances unlike any normal Alpha or Totem Pokemon could hope to achieve, which began to be seen as a bit of a blessing and also a curse as those Pokemon had the capability to do anything with that power that was given to them. While it's uncertain to where it came from, and whether or not it was intentional of Arceus or not to give them to non-legendaries, it was considered a blessing so far that not one single barer of the Crest of Arceus has used its powers for evil deeds. It could also be seen that these beings were able to live longer lifespans than most of their kind, albeit by an inconsistent measure differing between all species.

By the war's end, and society gradually beginning to prosper towards a brighter light than what it once was before, and most if not all problems the Pokemon once dealt with were mutually repaired with each other as the Legendaries guided all of the Pokemon towards a better resolution with each other and continued to mediate for the Pokemon for a while longer. The world was beginning to heal, and the land was beginning to repair with the aid of all of the pokemon working together, and after 50 years, the world was restored to a healthy state with poke-society thriving, and many a legendary pokemon being praised and respected for their righteous doings as guardians of the world.

Year: 1351, something began to become a semi-common sight for the people of each region; a growing number of random Pokemon on the planet becoming influenced and losing control of themselves to strange symptoms that almost make the mon seem possessed while they wreck havoc around and near their homes; areas where homes or villages once resided were suddenly taken over by suddenly appearing Mystery Dungeons that sealed their locales into a pocket dimension away from the rest of the world, only returning when a crazed Pokemon was defeated or when enough time had passed, but the latter has gone from upwards of a week to a few months.

It isn't until the year 1355 where it was officially announced to the full extent of what these cases of mania were. They were being influenced by up to 9 known different mental influences related to the 9 different vices that were recorded through the study: Anger/Wrath, Pride, Deceit, Envy, Avarice, Fear, Gluttony, Shamelessness, and Sloth. While it was unclear what caused these influences to start appearing and affecting a bunch of Pokemon, what was known was that it was likely to affect groups of people lead by a powerful leader featuring unique powers given to them by their connected Vice. Pokemon affected by these influences would show signs of overwhelming desire to fulfil their desired goals in relation to their given vice, and empowering those with them with a related effect. They are difficult to take down, but not impossible to be dealt with with teamwork, but some continue to gain power the longer they drown themselves within their desires, requiring more powerful aid with those connected to Arceus. Because of these outbursts, and the unconnected nature of where these influences came from, the effect of these powers have been labelled as: The Vanias; people who've lost control of themselves to the Vices, and seem to act in complete mania.

There have been documentations of individuals with similar powers to The Vanias, but with appear to have absolute control over these powers with no downsides; each individual having a unique control over a distinct aspect of themselves that align with their given vice, such as some of those with the Gluttony Vice being able to consume larger quantities of food within a day while being able to sustain themselves days without food because of the amount they've eaten, with some having the much stranger quality of being able to swallow creatures equal to or larger than themselves. Considering the powers can be controlled by two different types of individuals, but the root of the power comes from the same area of influence, their power has been titled as: The Power of Vices. To coin a name for these people, the Pokemon began to collectively call them Vicers.

Vicers are a rare breed; 1 in a million, but speculation doesn't stop people from assuming incorrectly about other Pokemon. The current tension between the Pokemon the Vicers are false reports of Vicers being the cause of the spread of and appearances of Vania, and due to the lack of actual evidence, rumours hold a tight grip on the truth and refuses to let go. Mystery Dungeon Guilds are doing all they can to research into the matter, but with the limited number of Vicers within their employ and the number of unknown Vicers out there that might be, it's very hard to contain the potential threats of Vicers who may be acting evil-y. Some Guilds are desperate enough to put out fliers hiring potential Vicers, in hopes that those with a good heart will put these powers to good use before something terrible occurs.

As of the year 1360, no large or ongoing cases of Vice Abuse or Vania have been reported, but this does not mean there haven't been any cases of these random appearances of Vania. That's where this campaign starts.

Power of Vices

A power unlike the Crests of Arceus; this power, possibly being born from the world's most terrible of sins, has given birth to a Hellish power that rises up from the cracks of the earth to infect the unfortunate populace with its influence, corrupting all it would touch as it spreads across the world and threatens to bring armageddon...is what most deeply fearful Pokemon are siting from other unhinged characters of the populace. The theories are endless; influence from Giratina; the corruption of Darkrai; the true instincts of Pokemon once lost now returning; our hidden selves that we summon power from like an alternate identity; the flying berry monster granting blessings to their chosen Pariahs; the list goes on and on and on. While nearly as few in number as the non-Legendary/Mythical Pokemon who've attained The Crest of Arceus, their number and mettle ranges far more than those who spawn with that sacred birthmark, and because of the stipulation behind Vania, most of the populace believe Vicers are the cause of the outbreaks of Vania, and without enough information or guaranteed sources to prove, Pokemon are going to continue believing in this invisible boogeymon until proven otherwise. What are you going to prove?

The Power of Vices is given at Level 1, and gains Levels up to 10 after defeating and absorbing the power of a corresponding defeated Vice user; Pokemon with Vania grant 1 Level, whilst Vicers provide Levels equal to their own Vice Level (E.g. A Level 2 Vicer beating a Level 2 Vicer and absorbing their power becomes a Level 4 Vicer instead of Level 3.)

When 2 or more Vicers with the same Vice battle a Pokemon with Vania and attempt to absorb its power, all members absorb the power equally due to the overwhelming amount of power that comes with an out of control Vice; when absorbing the power from a Vicer with the same Vice, their Level of Power is distributed equally amongst all Vicers with a Minimum of 1 Vice Level gained.

In a non-traditional campaign, The Power of Vices can scale up infinitely, but you can only gain levels up to the highest level above yours past 10 (E.g. Absorbing the Vice of a Level 13 Vice at Level 10, grants the new owner a Vice Level of 13, not 23.) The reason Level 10 is the normal limit, is because Vice Levels higher than 10 risk the Vicer's mind going berserk and being overwhelmed by their Vice.

Saving throws against Vice abilities are a DC of 10 + the Vice Level of the user.

(Optional Ruling) When trying to attain Levels of Vice above Level 10, make a Wisdom Save with a DC of 10 + the Level you're attempting to attain; whether you succeed or fail your Wisdom Save gives your next check, a Charisma Save, Advantage or Disadvantage with the same DC. Failing the DC causes the user to suffer a -1 penalty to all core stats for each attempt made to Level Up; Crit Failing causes the user to go berserk and attack all those around them until they fall unconscious.

Crests of Arceus (Optional, and I swear I wrote this idea before Silksong)

The legendary power that was granted by Arceus themselves to the Legendary Pokemon that roamed the earth, and those born by their desire to protect all on Earth. Once a power only granted to Legendaries and Mythicals, this power has been found amongst the populace with no pinpointed origin towards its happenstance; while few in number everywhere in-between, all non-Legendary/Mythical Pokemon granted this power have luckily been of the kind heart, doing what is needed to protect the world and peace. There is still fear however of one with this power going rogue, and rumours and restlessness don't help with the discomfort of the Pokemon still in harm's way. What will fate have in store for your Destiny?

The Crest comes in 3 Types, each providing a different benefit to its user:

- Type Crest: You gain a tertiary Typing that is supposed to statistically benefit you better against other Type Match-Ups. Alongside this is the ability to learn all Moves associated with that third Typing, but must be taught by a willing Teacher who can teach you it.
- Ability Crest: You gain any and all abilities associated with your species; Pokemon from mixed species families gain particularly strong benefits from this.
- Guardian Crest: You've gained the blessing of one of the many Legendary and Mythical Pokemon; you summon power similar to theirs into yourself, becoming an Avatar of their powers as you attain similar but weaker power.

Aside from each specific Crest's blessing, they Universally provide the following benefits to their user (It should be noted that Arceus Guardians benefit from double the bonus due to answering directly to Arceus):

- +5 to base AC before Armour and Dex
- +6 to Mental Saves
- +3 to Physical Saves
- +3 to Proficiency Bonus
- +10 to Religion and History checks in relation to information about Arceus' intervention in the Civil War, Arceus Guardians, Crests of Arceus, and worldly developments after the Civil War

New Moves

Add new Moves to your Move List at every Level Increment.

STAB Increase

STAB is an abbreviation for Same-Type Attack Bonus. Your Pokémon gains a bonus to damage rolls when it uses an attack move type that is the same as their Pokémon type. STAB may only be added once per move, even for moves that deal damage over time like Leech Seed, Smog, etc. This bonus increases as the Pokémon levels up.

Ability Score Improvement (ASI)

Your Pokémon gains a number of points to allocate to any of its ability scores, to a maximum of 20. The number of points to allocate depends on the number of evolutionary stages in that Pokémon's line:

- 1 stage: 4 points
- 2 stages: 3 points
- 3 stages: 2 points

Up to 2 of these points may be used to acquire a [Feat](https://poke5e.app/reference/feats) instead.

This happens at levels 4, 8, 12, 16, and 20.

Damage Increase

The damage of each damaging move increases at levels 5, 10, and 17, as shown in the move description.

Pokemon Paths

Like a Subclass, but with less total features, and stronger effects; please follow this list as it modifies it for a PMD experience; select 1 Path to follow at Level 2:

- Ace Fighter - Your goal is to become the strongest fighter in the world, and so you excel in battle. You gain +1 to your attack and damage rolls.

- [Lvl 5, Battle Master] When you reach Level 5, you gain a number of Battle Dice (d6) equal to 1 + your Dex Mod (Minimum of 1). You may add 1 of these dice to a single Attack or Damage Roll, after the result of the Initial Roll. You replenish your pool of Battle Dice at each long Rest.

Currency Conversion

Pokemon Moves

They are treated like Spells, placed into a Spell-listing, each with an independent PP value that's smaller than the OG's counterparts. Instead of limiting to 4 Moves only, you can gain all Moves you get Per-Level and use freely. Only in Competitions will there be a 4 Move Limit. Also all Pokemon can make Unarmed Strikes dealing 1d6+Str/Dex Bludgeoning, Piercing, or Slashing depending on the attack method; discuss with the DM how it's done, and size affects damage die.

STAB

STAB in this game is a bonus to Damage Rolls to Moves you share the same Typing with granted through the STAB section of the Pokemon Class Progression.

Switching Moves

Moves like U-Turn that switches your place with another Pokemon, I'll rule like a position-swap where you swap the place of yourself and a Pokemon of your choice in the Party. Also, Switching positions like this or any other way in the PMD universe does not undo combat buffs and debuffs.

Pokemon Held Items

Feat Progression

Normal for the most part; either get an ASI or replace Points for Feats. You can grab any normal Feats from WikiDot's 5e page, or from the list down below of modified Pokemon Feats specifically tailored for Pokemon Mystery Dungeon.

Vices and Crests

Crests are TOO OP for Level 1 Players, so they'll be saved for NPCs or other games in the future. Players will get access to the Vice System, and multiple Players can share the same Vice if they wish. Vices have their own levelling system that's not tied to EXP and are similar to Milestones. You gain Vice Feats every Vice Level you gain, and gain Universal Vice Feats at Level 1 and every odd Level.
